# Chapter 3: First Login Chaos — Outline

---

**Overall Chapter Goal**
Bring the world of Shiltz Online alive for the reader — not through exposition, but through Aruna experiencing it for the first time. The world is busy, a little absurd, and completely indifferent to the fact that she has no idea what she's doing. By the end of the chapter, the reader should feel like they've been to Hutan Silon.

**Emotional Arc**
Start: Overstimulated, a little comedic — the newbie spawn is chaos and Aruna is standing still in the middle of it.
Middle: Quiet wonder — Hutan Silon is the first time the world feels like a place rather than a loading screen.
End: Sucker-punched — literally. The Treasure Chest lands clean. The zone was built to teach. Rara refused to be taught.

**Scene Breakdown**
- Scene 1 — Desa Lime, Still Standing: Aruna hasn't moved. The world runs around her. Bentala arrives. Bima tries to explain the UI. Rara is not listening to the UI explanation.
- Scene 2 — First Steps, First Quest: Movement tutorial, more or less. The "Day 1: can't move properly" note earns its place. Bentala picks up a kill quest on Piya. They head toward Hutan Silon.
- Scene 3 — Approaching Hutan Silon: The establishing shot. From outside, Hutan Silon looks like a cluster of fat chicks wandering between trees. The narrator delivers this with full sincerity.
- Scene 4 — Inside Hutan Silon: First mob encounter. Piya introduced documentary-style. Fight is messy in a funny way. Moo Moo makes a brief appearance.
- Scene 5 — The Treasure Chest: Aruna spots it. Goes for it. Gets punched. Chapter ends not on defeat or drama — just on the specific feeling of getting punched by a loot box you were excited about.

**Key Character Moments**
- Bima/Bentala: guide mode, relaxed. Notices things about how Rara plays that he doesn't comment on yet.
- Rara/Aruna: present, curious, slightly chaotic. The chaos comes from how she approaches things, not from the game being hard.
- The Treasure Chest beat is Rara's chapter-closing character note: she saw it, she wanted it, she went for it.
